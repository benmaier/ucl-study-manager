# Background Reading: Data Analysis Methods

Please read through the following material on data analysis approaches relevant to the upcoming tasks.

## Working with Real-World Data

Real-world datasets are often messy. Common issues include:
- Missing or incomplete records
- Inconsistent formatting across data sources
- Duplicate entries or data entry errors
- Files that are irrelevant to your analysis
- Data that appears valid but has hidden quality problems

## Evaluating Data Quality

When working with multiple data sources, consider:
- **Coverage**: How complete is each dataset? Are there gaps?
- **Consistency**: Do different sources tell the same story?
- **Methodology**: How was the data collected? Could the collection method introduce bias?
- **Granularity**: Is the data at the right level of detail for your analysis?

## Statistical Considerations

When comparing data before and after an intervention:
- Look at both the direction and magnitude of change
- Consider whether the change is statistically significant
- Be cautious about drawing causal conclusions from observational data
- Always check whether confounding factors could explain the results

## Critical Thinking

- Question your assumptions
- Look for contradictions between data sources
- Consider alternative explanations for the patterns you observe
- Not all statistically significant results are meaningful, and not all important effects are statistically significant
